Yo. I am Julian DeGroot-Lutzner, a learner, maker, and explorer, from West Philadelphia. I am interested in using technology for social good in the civic space. I currently attend Pitzer College and study Computational Sociology and I was a 2018 Berktern. For fun, I run competitively on the Pomona-Pitzer cross-country and track teams, and I make art at the collaborative art center The Hive.

I took the opportunity of moving to California for college as a chance to explore the United States. During the last three summers, I have lived in Seattle, Denver, and Boston and worked at nonprofits and research centers. On my first free Saturday in a new city, I buy a cheap used bicycle and bike around all day. As I bike, I talk to locals, discover different neighborhoods, and gain a perspective for where I am living. The bike ride sets a standard for future exploration in my new home.

I learn, build, and create in a similar way to how I explore a new city: actively and curiously. I am not afraid to get my hands dirty and I learn best by testing theories and ideas out in practice. I like talking to people and working on projects outside of my comfort zone.
