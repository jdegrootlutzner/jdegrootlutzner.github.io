Yo. I am Julian DeGroot-Lutzner, a learner, maker, and explorer, from West Philadelphia. I currently study Computational Sociology at Pitzer College and I focus on the societal impacts of technology. I like solving problems in many formats and as a 2018 Berktern, I enfused my diverse skillset into my project Redial. For fun, I run competitively on the Pomona-Pitzer cross-country and track teams, and I make art at the collaborative art center The Hive.

I took the opportunity of moving to California for college as a chance to explore the United States. During the last three summers, I have lived in Seattle, Denver, and Boston and worked at nonprofits and research centers along the way. On my first free Saturday in a new city, I buy a cheap used bicycle and bike all over. As I bike, I talk to locals, discover different neighborhoods, and gain a perspective for where I am living. The bike ride sets a standard for future exploration in my new home.

I learn, build, and create in a similar way to how I explore a new city: actively and curiously. I am not afraid to get my hands dirty and I learn best by testing theories and ideas out in practice. I like talking to people and working on projects outside of my comfort zone and right now I am creating a new interactive art installation and video on digital privacy.

Please check out some of my projects on my website to learn more about my unique interdisciplinary background. If you have any questions, or just want to chat please drop me a line at jdegrootlutzner(at)gmail.com or on LinkedIn. Thanks!
